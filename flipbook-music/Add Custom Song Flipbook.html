Yes — but you can’t directly add your own custom song to that online FlippingBook link itself just by editing the link, because FlippingBook Online does not let you insert audio tracks into a published flipbook from the web interface alone. FlippingBook

Here’s what you can do depending on what access you have:

✅ Option 1 — If you own the flipbook source and can edit it in FlippingBook Publisher (desktop)

FlippingBook Publisher (the desktop tool used to create the flipbook) does allow adding a background audio track:

  1. Open your project in FlippingBook Publisher.

  2. Go to Settings → Controls.

  3. Find Sound → Customize.

  4. Upload your .mp3 file as the Background Sound File.

  5. Optionally adjust volume/loop. FlippingBook

Important limitations:

  • Only one audio track can be added.

  • It may start only after user interaction (first page turn) due to browser restrictions.

  • Background audio will not play automatically on most mobile devices. FlippingBook

After that, re-publish your flipbook — the public link will include the audio.

✅ Option 2 — Embed the flipbook in your own webpage and add audio outside of it

If you can embed the flippingbook in your website, you can layer audio outside the flippingbook player. For example:

html
<audio controls autoplay loop>
  <source src="YOUR-SONG.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
</audio>

<iframe
  src="https://online.flippingbook.com/view/660547826/"
  width="100%"
  height="800"
></iframe>

✅ This plays your song in the webpage while the flipbook shows below.
❗ It doesn’t embed audio inside the flipbook itself.

To embed: use the <iframe> code from FlippingBook’s embed options or create your own. FlippingBook

✅ Option 3 — Add audio via embedded interactive content (workaround)

If you can edit the flipbook pages (or embed it inside a page builder), you can:

  1. Host your audio track somewhere (SoundCloud, Google Drive link, MP3 URL, etc.)

  2. Insert a link or button inside the flipbook page that opens that audio (popup, external player, etc.)

  3. Or embed a tiny audio player widget near/around the flipbook on your site.

This doesn’t autoplay the audio inside the flipbook, but lets readers click to play.

❌ What won’t work

  • You cannot upload audio directly through the FlippingBook Online (web) interface. The audio setting exists only in the desktop Publisher app. FlippingBook

  • You cannot add per-page audio tracks inside an online flipbook; only a single background track is supported in Publisher. FlippingBook

⭐ If you want multiple tracks/audio per page

FlippingBook doesn’t support that. A common workaround is to embed short videos with sound (like MP4s) instead of audio files for specific pages — they’ll play when clicked. FlippingBook
